How Delvefall works

Delvefall is a shortcut. Type a card name and you get the cards that do the same thing, without learning a query language or knowing what any of it is filed under. It isn't a chatbot guessing either: it compares the printed rules text of every Magic card with plain math, and every number on the page is one you can check.

Everything below is optional. The site works if you only ever type card names.

Two kinds of similar, counted together

Every result is scored twice, and the two scores are averaged.

  • rules text: cards whose printed text works the same way. Search Sol Ring, get the other rocks that tap for two.
  • concepts: cards built around the same idea, from community tags. Search Sol Ring, get the whole mana-rock family.
  • The badge on every card is the average of the two, and the list is always in order of the number you see. Hover it to break the number back into its halves.

There used to be a slider here to weight one against the other. It is gone, because testing it settled the question: an even split beats every other setting, including both ends. Either one alone is a specialist's view, and the two together catch what neither does on its own.

Weaker matches aren't thrown away. The button under the results opens them ten percentage points at a time, saying which range it is about to show, and each batch is labelled where it starts. That is deliberate: it keeps the sorts honest. Sorting a single band by price answers "what is the cheapest card that matches this well", where sorting everything below the line at once would just hand you the cheapest card in the game with a faint resemblance.

Filters

The wide box in the filter bar takes scryfall style syntax and applies it to the results (the search box itself only takes card names). -o:sacrifice usd<=5 keeps results cheap and free of sacrifice clauses. The whole vocabulary:

  • o:word rules text contains it, o:"draw a card" for phrases
  • t:legendary the type line must contain this, any word works
  • id:wu colour identity (fits-within), id:c colourless
  • otag:removal the community tags, served from this site's own daily mirror of scryfall's tagger data
  • usd<5 eur>=1 gbp<2 price, price<5 whichever currency the switch shows, mv<=2 cmc=4 mana value
  • is:dfc two-sided cards, is:split is:mdfc is:saga and other layouts, is:gamechanger the commander watchlist
  • f:commander legal in commander, banned:commander the reverse (commander is the only format tracked)
  • a - negates anything (-t:creature means no creatures), words side by side mean and, or and parentheses group: (o:draw or o:scry) -t:creature

That's the whole vocabulary, not all of scryfall's. There's no pow/tou, rarity, set, artist, format beyond commander, or regex here yet, the cards table just doesn't carry those fields. Anything the box doesn't recognise is skipped rather than erroring, so a stray word never breaks the search.

Where the pound price comes from

Scryfall quotes two currencies, dollars and euros, so pounds are worked out here rather than looked up. That is why the picker says approximate, and it is worth knowing what the number is before you trust it to a budget.

  1. Both of a card's prices are converted to pounds at the day's reference rates (the European Central Bank's, fetched once a day).
  2. The two results are averaged. They rarely agree, because the American and European markets price cards differently, and the middle is the least wrong single answer.
  3. If a card has only one of the two prices, that one is used on its own. If it has neither, it has no pound price, and any price filter hides it.

So a pound figure is a fair estimate of what a card is worth, not a price anyone is actually asking. For a real number, check a UK seller. Dollars and euros are printed exactly as scryfall reports them.

Unique cards

The unique page is the opposite of search: it deals you cards that do something no other card really does, always from the most unique ones you haven't seen yet. Where several are about equally unique it picks between them at random, so your run is your own rather than a fixed list everybody walks in the same order. It has the same slider and filters. On the rules text end you get (hopefully interesting) abilities, on the concepts end you get cards whose tag profile matches nothing else. The arrows walk back through everything you've been dealt, across visits.

Where this is going

Delvefall is a small site with a long to-do list. Roughly what is coming, in the order it is likely to arrive:

  • Searching one ability, sharpened. Clicking a line of rules text already narrows the search to that ability. The work in progress is making the concepts side follow the same line, so picking "when this enters" stops dragging in everything else the card happens to do.
  • Pages that map the card pool. The uniqueness data can answer questions nothing else can: which cards read ordinarily but do something odd, which concepts the game has barely explored, which cards are functional twins. Those become browsable pages.
  • Maybe a deck lens. Paste a list, see the alternatives to each card and which ones have no real substitute at all. Further off, and only if the browsing above turns out to be the smaller half of what people want.

Found a bad match, or missing a card you expected? The report link on any results page is the fastest way to change what happens next: those reports become the test cases the matcher has to pass.
